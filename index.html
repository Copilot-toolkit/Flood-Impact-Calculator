Important Limitations

This tool provides illustrative estimates to frame the potential scale of flood exposure at a site level. It is not a formal business interruption review, flood risk assessment, or insurance valuation. Users should be aware of the following simplifications:

Business interruption is modelled on a flat daily gross profit basis. It does not account for seasonality, peak trading periods, increased cost of working, salvage, supply chain disruption, or contractual penalties — all of which may materially affect actual BI exposure.

Physical damage allocation across property fabric, moveable contents, and fixed assets is based on illustrative profiles (Low / Medium / High) rather than site-specific asset surveys. Actual exposure will depend on flood depth, duration, contamination, and the specific assets present.

Mitigation assumptions (BI duration reduction, contents salvage rates, property and fixed asset protection) are indicative estimates based on the principle that advance warning enables pre-emptive action. They are not derived from a specific claims dataset and should be validated against real deployment outcomes.

Flood probability bands are broad approximations used to annualise expected loss. They do not replace a site-specific flood risk assessment informed by hydraulic modelling and local flood history.

Sector closure benchmarks are informed by published academic research and UK industry surveys but represent cross-sector averages with wide variance. Individual site outcomes depend on factors including flood severity, asset complexity, supply chain dependencies, and business continuity preparedness.

For a full business interruption review, a site-specific flood risk assessment, or modelled expected annual loss values, please contact your insurance broker or risk advisor.
